# Storyboard Prompt

A professional 3x3 storyboard grid showing 9 sequential cinematic frames. Frame 1 must be a direct visual continuation of the attached reference image.

**Story:**

The sequence unfolds as follows: [YOUR STORY]

---

## Layout & Composition

**Grid Specification:** Generate a single image containing a perfect 3x3 storyboard grid (9 frames total). Each frame must be separated by a thin, clean white border.

**Dynamic Cinematography:** Vary the camera's perspective across the 9 frames. Use a mix of wide establishing shots, medium character shots, and tight close-ups. Avoid repeating the same composition or "camera" height in consecutive panels.

**Lens Accuracy:** Maintain a consistent depth of field that matches the reference image (e.g., shallow focus if the background is blurred).

---

## Narrative & Pacing

**The 4-Beat Arc:** Sequence the 9 frames into a cohesive story arc:

- **Frames 1–2 (Setup):** Establish the environment and character state.
- **Frames 3–5 (Rising Tension):** Introduce a conflict or movement.
- **Frames 6–7 (Climax):** The peak moment of action or discovery.
- **Frames 8–9 (Resolution):** The immediate aftermath or consequence.

**Incipient Action:** Each frame must capture the incipient moment of a beat—depict the split second before the action reaches its peak to create a sense of cinematic anticipation.

---

## Visual Fidelity & Continuity

**Absolute Continuity:** Enforce strict visual DNA across the entire grid. The character's appearance, wardrobe, props, lighting temperature, and environmental details must remain identical in every frame.

**Hyper-Realistic Style:** Render in a high-end photographic style. Use 35mm cinematic lighting, physically accurate textures (skin, fabric, metal), and realistic shadows.

**Zero Text Overlay:** Do not include any captions, subtitles, frame numbers, or watermarks. Only text that would naturally exist within the physical scene (e.g., a neon sign or a book cover) is permitted.

---

## Image: The Grid Architecture

**Format:** A single 16:9 high-definition image containing a precise 3×3 storyboard grid.

**Separation:** Each of the 9 panels must be divided by a minimalist thin white border to ensure a clean, professional aesthetic.

**Negative Constraints:** The image must remain purely visual. Strictly no text overlays, captions, frame numbers, or watermarks. The panels should speak for themselves through composition and lighting.

---

## Text: The Motion Shot List

**Structure:** Provide a separate, numbered list (Frames 1–9) consisting of one Motion Prompt per panel.

**The "Incipient Action" Rule:** Each prompt must describe the first 2–4 seconds of movement that would occur if the frame were the first image of a video clip.

**Prompt Engineering:**

- Start with the subject's immediate physical action.
- Include specific camera movement (e.g., "The camera slowly dollies in as…" or "A subtle handheld jitter follows…").
- Focus on the momentum—describe the energy and direction of the scene as it breaks from the static frame into a living moment.
